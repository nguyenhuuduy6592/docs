# 🍼 Baby Food Guide — HIA Doha Transit (Hanoi → Edinburgh)

## 🛫 Your Route & Terminal Context

**Hanoi (HAN) → Doha (DOH) → Edinburgh (EDI)** — All on **Qatar Airways**, transiting through **Hamad International Airport (HIA)**.

**Key facts about HIA:**
- HIA has **one single terminal** with 5 concourses: **A, B, C, D, E**
- All concourses are connected airside — you can walk between them freely after security
- Qatar Airways departures to/from Southeast Asia (Hanoi) typically use **Concourse D or E**
- Qatar Airways departures to UK/Europe (Edinburgh) typically use **Concourse B or C**
- During a 2h40 transit with ~1 hour to buy, **Concourse C is your sweet spot** — it's the largest and centrally connected, and you should head there first

> ⚠️ **Important note:** HIA is a single-terminal airport. You do NOT need to re-clear security between concourses. Once airside, all shops are accessible to you regardless of which gate you arrive at or depart from. Head directly to Concourse C/B for the best options.

## 👶 Additional Important Tips for Your Transit

**For your 18-month-old specifically:**

1. **Best foods to order:** Plain steamed/boiled rice, rice congee/porridge (Madrouba at Daily Chef Noof is ideal), udon noodles (Wagamama), soft donburi rice bowl (YO! Sushi), soft naan/bread, mild lentil soup.

2. **Always ask the kitchen:** Even if the menu doesn't list a plain option, most Asian restaurants at airports will make plain steamed rice on request. Ask for "plain rice, no sauce, no seasoning" for the baby.

3. **Carry-on food rules:** You CAN carry cooked food through airport security and onto the plane (EU/Qatar rules generally allow this). Pack leftover rice or bread in a sealed container to use during the Edinburgh flight.

4. **Bring your own utensils:** Pack a small spoon and baby bowl — airport plastic spoons are often too large for 18-month-olds.

5. **Hydration:** At 18 months without formula, ensure he stays well hydrated. **Airport Juice Bar (Concourse C)** can make fresh fruit smoothies, or grab bottled water. Avoid sugary juices.

6. **Timing:** Don't wait until you're hungry — buy food within the first 30–40 minutes of your transit. Restaurants can be slow. Use the takeaway/grab-and-go options where possible.

7. **Qatar Airways Family Assistance:** If you are traveling with Qatar Airways, inform the crew on board — they may have soft foods or plain rice available as part of their baby/child meal service (pre-order SPML - Special Meal for children before the flight if you haven't already).

8. **Stroller/Pram:** HIA has pram/stroller facilities. Use the Family Services desk if you need a pram for transit.

9. **Nursing/Family Room:** HIA has dedicated family rooms and baby-changing facilities in all concourses — useful if you need to feed him in a calm environment.

10. **Don't miss your gate:** Edinburgh-bound gates are often called 45–60 minutes before departure. Set a timer to be back at your gate at least 30 minutes before boarding.
